ATOMIC FORCE MICROSCOPY (AFM)

An atomic force microscope measures properties such as height, friction
and magnetism of small samples such as atoms. The microscope uses a
probe to go over the surface and measure the force between it and the sam-
ple. The data collected is then used to determine the properties of the
sample.

Source:http://www.nanoscience.gatech.edu/zlwang/research/afm.html

As the cantilever oscillates across the sample, the force between the probe
and the surface is measured and translated to the graph below. In this specific
graph, the heights of the sample are recorded which allows us to determine the
shape of the sample.
